Example 1.2.0.31.

Simple random sample. This is the easiest method to study.
Ahmed hopes to assemble a 5-person research team (himself and four others) from his microeconomics course, which has 28 students excluding Ahmed. To select a simple random sample of size four from the other class members, Ahmed could enter all 28 names into a spreadsheet. We propose different options
  1. Options 1) Randomize the order, close his eyes, and take the first four names.
  2. Option 2) Ahmed to first record the last names of the classmates along with a twi-digit number, generate random numbers, and create pairs associated to each ID, first group consists of the first four-pairs found by the random numbers, second group similarly, and so on. Such as 0.901245, it has 90, 01, 12, 24, 45. Where 01, 12, and 24 will correspond to three numbers in the list. To get more numbers, we could generate more random numbers. (Spreadsheet (restricted access)),
  3. Option 3) Each student should enumerate from 0-1 or 1-4. It works with other elements such as animals, and letters with a sequence.
