Standard deviation. It is a measure of variability. Mathematically, the standard deviation is
\begin{equation*} \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{1}{N-1} \sum\limits^{N}_{i=1}(x_i - \overline{x})^2} \end{equation*}
and it roughly describes how far away the typical observation is from the mean. Usually, one standard deviation means the probability that every observation will be within one sigma band is \(68.5\%.\) Two standard deviation means the probability that every observation will be within two sigma band is \(95.5\%\) and so on. Notice The deviation is the distance of the observation from its mean, namely, \(x_i - \overline{x}\text{.}\) For instance, \(9.5 - 10 = -0.5\) is the deviation.
