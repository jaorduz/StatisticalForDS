    Section 1.2 Sampling

    Objectives

    • Introduce concepts and correct terminology to organize data.
    This section shows sampling concepts and more.
    Data must be organized and descripted, this is relevant in any analysis.
    How are we going to call a database? To use a common name could leave lack of clarity. Therefore, people use some characteristics of the database.
    • The first word in the first column
    • The number of rows/columns
    This is an informal way to talk about columns, rows, database and more. Then, we proceed to formalize some relevant concepts.
    • Variables are the formal name to identify columns in a database. This variables represent the Characteristics for each row.
    • Case is the word to call each row. AKA observational unit.
    • Data Matrix is the way to organize data. This is convenient to put in a spreadsheet.

    Definition 1.2.0.4. Types of variables.

    Variable can share characteristics, but each variables is different in terms of meaning, shape and representation.
    Differences are important, and it means it is not possible to operate with mathematical symbols, or add extra information is not possible such area code, or ZIP-Code. In addition, some amounts require discrete numbers, and talk about decimal (continuous) numbers in, for example, population is not possible. On the other hand, some variables can bre represented by acronymous such as state, e.g. IN, NY, CA, etc.

    Let us talk about a relationship between variables. Most researcher look for relationship between variables. And similarly, we start our questions and variables are in (scientific) questions.
    • How far is Sun from Earth?
    • What is the probability to obtain a kind of particle in a decay process?
    • What is power consumption bill for a family with four members?
    In general, resesearchers want to figure out relationships between variables, and to do it, numerical, and graphical analysis are relevant.
    When a researcher finds a relationship then variables are associated Variables are also known as dependant Variables.

    Remark 1.2.0.16.

    Database can be analyzed from different ways and it will open the fact to have more questions. To do this, we have other definitions:
    • Negative association out the trend in a scatterplot.
    • Positive association in the trend in a scatterplot
    • Independent variables no evident relationship between two variables.
    • Explanatory variables these kind of variables (probably) affect another. AKA independent variable.
    • Response variables these kind of variable (probably) will be affected by the response variables. AKA dependent variable.
    • Observational study It means a study to collect data without interfiring how the data arise.
    • Experiments Situations where researchers explore a potential causal connection.
    • Randomized experiment It is the random assignation of individuals in groups to test the explanatory or response variables.
    • Association It means a correlation between variables, but it does not imply causation.
    • Causation It is inferred from random experiments.

    Sampling and other concepts.
    How are data collected? It is important to achieve objectives.

    Remark 1.2.0.19.

    What do population and sample mean? To stablish a question, we inherently think in a population. This population has several features, and requirements. We will call the population or target population. Every member in the population stands for a case. Since collecting each case usually is too expensive, so we consider a sample.
    The following Definition¬†1.2.0.20 is relevant in our course.

    Definition 1.2.0.20.

    Sample it is a subset of cases and represents a small fraction of population.

    Sampling, population and bias

    Definition 1.2.0.25.

    • Bias the fact which permits data manipulation with or without intention of favoring any output.
    • Simple random sample Is the most basic random sample that introduces ramdomness, namely, Sampling randomly to avoid any particular benefit.
    The act of taking Simple random sample helps to minimize the bias.

    • Convenience sample is the fact of including the cases that are easily accessible then they are more likely to be included in the sample. is the fact of including the cases that are more likely to be included in the sample and they are easily accessible.
    Observational Studies
    We start with some definitions

    Definition 1.2.0.27.

    • Observational data this is how we call data wich don‚Äôt have any applied treatment. Observational studies are sufficcient to show associations or stablish hypothesis to be verified trought different experiments.
    • Observational studies have two forms: Prospective and Retrospective. The first one identifies individuals and collects information as event unfold. The retrospective study collects data after events happened.
    • Confounding variable is a variable which correlated with both explanatory and response variables. AKA lurking, confounder factor, or confounder.
    We intruduce the following sampling techniques in order to assure the randomness and the statistical validity.

    Definition 1.2.0.29.

    • Simple sampling It was defined on Definition¬†1.2.0.25 such as the most basic random sample that introduces ramdomness. It is the most intuitive form of random sampling. AKA Simple random.
    • Stratified sampling The population is divided in groups called Strata. It is useful when each Stratum are very similar with respect to outcome of interest.
    • Cluster sampling The populaion is divided in groups called Clusters. Then we sample a fixed number of clusters and include all observations from each of those clusters in the sample.
    • Multistage sampling is similar to cluster sample, but we collect a random sample within each selected cluster.
