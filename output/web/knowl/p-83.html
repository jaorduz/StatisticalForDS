  • Mean AKA Average It is one of the most popular way to measure the center of a distribution.
    \begin{gather} \overline{x} = \sum\limits_i^n \frac{x_i}{n}\tag{1.3.1} \end{gather}
    It is the sample mean, where \(x_i\) represents the \(i-\)th observed value.
  • Population mean and Sample mean can be calculated trough the same way, using the \(\mu = \sum\limits_i^N \frac{x_i}{N}.\) Notice, the population mean is represented by \(\mu.\)
  • Weighted mean. Essentially, mean and weighted mean are same, except that the last one has an impact if the number of observations change.
    \begin{gather} \text{Weighted mean of } x_i= \frac{\sum\limits^{n}_{i=1} w_i x_i}{\sum\limits^{n}_{i=1} w_i}\tag{1.3.2} \end{gather}
    where \(w_i\)’s are the weights, and \(x_i\)’s are the observations. Notice the eqs. (1.3.1) and (1.3.2) are same if in the last one \(w_1=w_2=w_3= \dots = w_n = 1\)
  • Mode. It is the prominent peak in the distribution (sometimes it is identified as the highest rectangle) in the histogram. Besides, it is the value with the most occurrences in the data set. But this definition has some issues, such as, it is uncommon to have repetitions in the data set. On the other hand, the data set can have more peaks, e.g. one mode (unimodal), two mode (bimodal), or several modes (multimodal).
  • Standard deviation. It is a measure of variability. Mathematically, the standard deviation is
    \begin{equation*} \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{1}{N-1} \sum\limits^{N}_{i=1}(x_i - \overline{x})^2} \end{equation*}
    and it roughly describes how far away the typical observation is from the mean. Usually, one standard deviation means the probability that every observation will be within one sigma band is \(68.5\%.\) Two standard deviation means the probability that every observation will be within two sigma band is \(95.5\%\) and so on. Check the figure 2  (check the website blog 3 ). Notice The deviation is the distance of the observation from its mean, namely, \(x_i - \overline{x}\text{.}\) For instance, \(9.5 - 10 = -0.5\) is the deviation.
  • Variance. It is a measure of variability. It is called as the second central moment of the sample.
    \begin{align} \sigma^2 &= \frac{1}{N-1} \sum\limits^{N}_{i=1}(x_i - \overline{x})^2\tag{1.3.3} \end{align}
    Note the eq. (1.3.3) is the definition of variance. The variance is the average squared distance from the mean. Notice the variance symbol is \(\sigma^2.\)
